Some Projects
That I've built

Just a small description of some of the projects that I've worked on over the years. I hope you find them pretty cool!

November, 2014

USC Eats
for Android

USC has a lot of cafeterias, which is nice since it means theres some food diverstiy. However, as an android user it was a challenge since the webpage was not mobile optimized and there was no equivalent to the iOS Eats app. So, I built my own, to help android users (like myself), have an easier time deciding where to eat.

October, 2016

Azobo
mobile app

Azobo was an idea my friend and I had, and hacked out a basic proof of concept at HackPoly. The idea was to make a yelp-like app, but to which any user could upload their content, so that underrepresented gems like street art would have a place to get discovered.

November, 2016

Blackfin
Communications

Blackfin communications was a data visualization tool, that made it easy for shareholders in the global supply chain to provide data to those who needed it.

June, 2017

Globe
Voice Controlled Maps

Globe was a project my team and I built for Santa Barbara hacks, which made it possible to communicate with maps via voice, and interact with geographic data handsfree. The map would respond to commands like "Go to Tokyo", and "Show me meteorite landings", as well as being motion controlled.

July, 2017

Faultier
Marketing Automation

Faultier is a project that mines data from social media websites and the internet to track down people that fit a target demographic, to make it easier for people without the time or budget for serious marketing to discover potential users and validate their ideas.

July, 2017

Audiate
Playlist Sharing

Audiate provides a platform for people to both share playlists from any site, be it soundcloud, itunes, or spotify, as well as letting people compete and vote on the best playlists by genre!
